Lisbeth Salander and the Punk Renaissance in Mainstream Women's Fashion
Jenna Adrian-Diaz

Keywords:punk/punk subculture/gender/feminism/body modification

Abstract

This paper will explore how Rooney Mara’s portrayal of the character Lisbeth Salander in David Fincher’s The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo, along with the work of costume designer Trish Summerville, led to a rebirth of punk elements in contemporary women’s fashion. The characterization and costuming of Lisbeth Salander made the concept of dress as armor, utility, and expression alluring to consumers, designers, and retailers prevalent in both fast-fashion and couture. In this paper I will discuss how the image of a girl on the fringes of society permeated socioeconomic barriers to sell staples of punk style to millions.
